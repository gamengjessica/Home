What Is The Mandela Effect

The Mandela Effect are memories of which mutiple people share except, these memeries are all fake. It’s where a large number of people remember the same thing or situation, but in reality, it was never real. Looking into all the examples we have, some of the theories, and the amount of people that have experienced this mandela effect, has borught life to this interesting phenomenon.

The Mandela Effect’s Origin

This wonder came to be in the year 2009 When Fiona Broome had made a website of her own, to talk about this so called “Mandela Effect”. Fiona was at a meeting having a conversation about the death of the south afrrican president who had passed away in prison, back in the 1980s. Everyone with Fiona had remembered this exact event happening. They all said they saw it on the News. They later found out that president Nelson Mandela had not died and instead passed in 2013. Fiona was stunned on the fact that so many people had remembred the exact same event while the event had never even existed. She called this the “Mandela Effect” and created a website where she would elaborate on this occurence and other events like this one. She called it the MandelaEffectSite.
